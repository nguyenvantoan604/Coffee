Vietnamese Coffee (Phin): This method is popular in Southeast Asian countries. Coffee is brewed by placing a filter (a device with a mesh filter) on top of a cup, adding coffee grounds to the filter, and slowly pouring hot water into the filter. This process creates a rich and flavorful cup of coffee. Espresso: This method involves using an espresso machine. High pressure is applied to finely ground coffee beans, resulting in a short, concentrated shot of coffee with a smooth layer of crema on top. Pour-over: This method requires slowly pouring hot water over coffee grounds through a filter. The water flows through the filter, absorbing the flavors, and drips into a cup below, creating a pure and enjoyable cup of coffee. French Press: This method uses a pot and a plunger with a mesh filter to steep coarsely ground coffee with hot water. The plunger is then pressed down to separate the coffee grounds from the liquid, resulting in a rich and robust cup of coffee.
